Problem Description

Podcast, formed by combining “iPod” and “broadcast”, is an episodic series of audio files which a user can download to listen on mobile devices. Right now, there are more than 70 thousand active podcast episodes and over 29 million episodes, including content in more than 100 languages. 32% of the US population, around 105 million people, listen to podcasts at least every month.
If you want to be a podcaster, once you’ve launched your podcast, having a website for your show would be a good idea to promote your podcast to the millions of people who might be interested in tuning in. On the website, listeners can discover new episodes, learn more about your content, and react to the show. However, there isn’t any website configuration and template specifically designed for podcasts. Podcasters only can choose WordPress, which is complicated, cumbersome, originally designed for presenting visual content, to build their website. Also, purchasing a ready-made theme for podcast might be necessary for most of them who don’t have coding experience. Obviously, Podcasters need a platform which is free, simple, lightweight, and exclusively designed for podcast, to promote their show.

App Description

CastPress(working title) is a web app which enables podcast hosts simply build and update their website and where podcast listeners could easily browse and play their favorite episodes.
The app provides a clean backstage interface for podcasters to upload their latest sound files with cover images and customize the appearance of website, supporting multiple authors and adding tags in sound timeline. On the other hand, the audience could browse a minimalist forestage website, which is purely designed for playing podcasts. They are able to use different search filters, including release date, keywords, and tags to reach their desired episodes and share them on social media platforms with attractive web page preview.

App Functionality

If you are a podcaster and this is your website, you can click a small icon in the coner on your homepage to login to the backstage. In the backstage, you can change the color of your website's background, text, and button in the appearance section, or click the plus icon to create a new post in the episodes section. Before posting a new episode, you have to upload a cover images and your sould files, enter description and links to other podcast player, such as iTunes and Spotify, and add tags to the post or sound timeline as an option. Once you've enteted all the required information, click "Send" and you will see the post on the homepage.
